I looked at your site, **Touchline 26 | Live World Cup Analytics**. The good news is that you're not building "another scores app." You're building something much more interesting: a **World Cup data visualization platform**. ([world-cup-command-center.jose-troche-coder.workers.dev][1])

For a solo developer, I would not try to compete with apps like [FotMob](https://www.fotmob.com?utm_source=chatgpt.com) or [Sofascore](https://www.sofascore.com?utm_source=chatgpt.com) on scores. I would focus on **shareable insights**.

## The Core Principle

People do not share:

* Schedules
* Scores
* Standings

People do share:

* Predictions
* Rankings
* Surprises
* Visualizations that make them look smart

The most successful World Cup apps grow around major match moments, not around generic traffic. Demand spikes before matches, after upsets, and around prediction content. ([ASO World][2])

# Automation #10: The Viral Engine

The loop:

```text
Match Happens
    ↓
Analytics Update
    ↓
Image Generated
    ↓
Auto Post
    ↓
Clicks
    ↓
Website
    ↓
Email Signup
```

The most effective automated posts are usually:

1. Win probabilities
2. Advancement probabilities
3. Upset rankings
4. Power rankings
5. "What Changed?" summaries

Those are data products, not news. ESPN, FIFA, and most score apps focus on reporting events. Your advantage is explaining what those events mean mathematically and visually. That's the content most likely to be shared automatically throughout the tournament.
